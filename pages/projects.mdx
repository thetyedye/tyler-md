# Projects

Things I'm building. Day-job project first, then personal stuff.

## Dato Agent
*Status: building · Strategy Labs · day job · part of The Lab*

Dato is an internal agent for the Strategy Labs team — the team-facing surface of The Lab. One place to query everything the agency runs on: verified marketing data across all clients, plus the company knowledge that gives it meaning — workflows, playbooks, client vaults, call transcripts, decisions.

It lives wherever the team already works: Slack, Claude Code, Cowork, Codex, any agent harness. Anyone at the agency can query our verified marketing data from any spot, and get a cited, source-backed answer — without opening a dashboard, rebuilding a report, or asking the person who remembers.

## The Lab
*Status: building · Strategy Labs · day job*

Strategy Labs' product studio — the umbrella that lets us take new tools to market fast without the weight of a full brand launch. Internal code names, beta with friendlies, iterate on real feedback, kill what doesn't work. Closest reference is [Every](https://every.to).

Current products under the umbrella include Signal (call analysis), Benchmarking (cross-brand performance comparison), and Uno Mas Food Costing. Dato is the team-internal agent that sits underneath.

*Status: ongoing · personal*

My portable agent core. Identity, skills, and routing rules — same agent across Telegram, Claude Code, and anywhere else I plug it in. "Thin harness, fat skills": decision logic lives in readable markdown skill files, not buried in code. Swappable harnesses, no vendor lock-in.

## TBrain
*Status: ongoing · personal*

My personal Obsidian knowledge graph second brain. Three layers: `raw/` for frictionless daily capture, `sources/` for curated external material, `wiki/` for compiled knowledge with cross-linked entities (people, companies, projects, ideas, tools). Every fact has a source. Brain-first: my agents check here before reaching for an external API.
