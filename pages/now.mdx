# Now

*What I'm actually focused on right now.*

## Day job

I'm a **Marketing Data Specialist** at **Strategy Labs**, handling reporting buildouts, data management, and ETL platforms. Recently transitioned into building AI systems, agents, workflows, and tools. My goal for this next chapter is transforming companies to become AI-native — what that looks like on both the communication side and the technical side.

## The Lab

The Lab is Strategy Labs' product studio — an umbrella that lets us move quickly without the weight of a full brand launch. We run early tools under boring internal names, beta with friendlies and existing clients, iterate fast, and kill ideas that don't get traction without it feeling like a failed launch.

Products currently under The Lab:

- **Signal** — call analysis dashboard. Sentiment, commitments, time tracking, surfacing missed opportunities. Built a prototype this weekend.
- **Benchmarking** — cross-brand performance comparison, built for brands.
- **Uno Mas Food Costing** — noodling on it, no real team resources needed yet.

When one of these earns its way out (real users, real confidence), it gets spun out with its own brand, domain, and GTM. Until then it lives in The Lab.

## What I'm building

- **Dato** — the internal Strategy Labs agent, part of The Lab. Custom narrow harness on the Pi SDK, read-only by design, live in Slack with a select team. Queries marketing data across all clients plus company knowledge (workflows, playbooks, transcripts) in one place. Currently expanding from data queries into context retrieval.
- **Noko** — my personal agent assistant. Always-on, voice-first, runs on the same `tyler-agent` + `tyler-brain` core I use for everything else.
- **This site** — turning it into a real career artifact and source of truth for the agents I work with.

## Driving adoption

Building the tools is half of it. The other half is getting the team to actually use them. I'm driving AI adoption across Strategy Labs — building skills people reach for, sitting with teammates to find where an agent fits their real work, educating, and communicating what's possible without overselling it. The human-to-human side of going AI-native: translating between what the tools can do and what people actually need.

## Learning

Agent workflows, how agents operate within a business, AI-native transformations, AI SDKs, and data infrastructure for agents to query easily.
